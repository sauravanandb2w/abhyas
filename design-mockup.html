Adarsh — design rationale

Name

Adarsh (आदर्श) = "ideal" / "model" in Sanskrit and Hindi. The app is a vault of model answers from toppers. The word also carries the meaning "exemplar" — what you aspire to. Looks great in Source Serif 4, easy to say, distinctive.

Tokens (Anthropic light + paper-mode shifts)

#faf7ed bg #ffffff card #cc785c accent #d97757 saffron

Paper stripe colors (the thin bar at top of each paper card): GS1 #c97d5a · GS2 #7d8f6f · GS3 #6b8194 · GS4 #a87b95 · Essay #8a8265 · Optional #b89149. Six muted, distinguishable shades to visually identify papers without relying on text alone.

Three-screen flow

1. Home / Browse — six paper cards in 2×3, recent answers strip, search at top. Single entry point.
2. Paper detail — sub-topics as collapsible rows. Click a sub-topic → expands with individual questions. Each question shows which toppers answered it (color pills).
3. Answer detail — question card on top, topper card with rank/marks/year/college, big scanned answer image (zoomable), prev/next navigation between answers for same question.

Bottom nav · 4 destinations

Browse (default home) · Saved (your starred answers) · Recent (last 20 viewed) · Toppers (browse by topper instead of paper — e.g., "all answers by Shruti Sharma AIR 1 2021").

Information architecture

Each answer has metadata: topper name, AIR (rank), CSE year, college/background, optional subject, marks awarded, time taken, page count, question text, paper, sub-topic, tags. This metadata is what makes the app powerful — search/filter on any field.

What makes this different from PDFs floating around the internet

• Structured by topic, not by topper — you find the right answer for what you're studying NOW.
• Multiple toppers per question — compare approaches.
• Searchable across question text + topper name + year.
• Bookmarks + recent — your personal study trail.
• Side-by-side with Bholwa / CA / PYQ — one ecosystem under your Google login.

Data sources (provided by you)

You said you'll provide topper copy data. The app expects: a folder of scanned PNG/JPEG images + a JSON metadata file with the structure above. We can also support: PDF per answer (split into images at upload), bulk upload via dashboard, or paste-from-URL.

Tech stack (proposed — matches your other apps)

Single-file HTML/JS · Supabase for metadata + bookmarks · GitHub for images (free, your existing OAuth flow already authorizes uploads) · Netlify auto-deploy from git. All login via the same Gmail you already use.

Build estimate (after your approval)

~4–6 hours of focused work for v1. Includes: data structure design, three screens, image viewer with zoom, search, bookmarks, Supabase schema, bulk upload helper. Sync with your existing ecosystem.
